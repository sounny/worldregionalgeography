Physical Geography

Mainland vs. Insular: The region divides into mainland countries (Myanmar, Thailand, Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia) and insular countries (Indonesia, Philippines, Malaysia's eastern portion, Singapore, Brunei, Timor-Leste).

Strategic Waterways: The Strait of Malacca and South China Sea are among the world's busiest shipping lanes, critical for global trade.

Biodiversity: Tropical rainforests host extraordinary biodiversity, though deforestation for palm oil threatens these ecosystems.

üåê Global Theme: Globalization and Trade

Southeast Asia is a major manufacturing hub for global supply chains. ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations) promotes economic integration and regional cooperation.

The region balances between Chinese and American influence while seeking its own path to development.

Texas Connection

Manufacturing and Trade

Electronics, textiles, and manufactured goods from Southeast Asia fill Texas stores. Texas ports handle significant trade with the region, and Vietnamese and Filipino communities are among the largest Asian populations in Texas.
