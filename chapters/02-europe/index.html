Europe

A peninsula of peninsulas, Europe has exerted global influence far beyond its size. From the Industrial Revolution to the European Union, this region offers crucial lessons in integration and cultural diversity.

Physical Geography

Major Physical Regions

The Northern European Lowland: A flat plain stretching from France through Germany and Poland. Major rivers including the Rhine and Elbe cross this region.

The Alpine System: Young, folded mountains including the Alps, Pyrenees, and Carpathians, created by plate collision.

The Scandinavian Shield: Ancient rocks scraped by glaciers, featuring fjords and numerous lakes.

Climate

Europe's climate is moderated by the North Atlantic Drift, keeping Western Europe warmer than other regions at similar latitudes.

üåê Global Theme: Supranationalism

The European Union (EU) represents the world's most ambitious experiment in supranationalism.

Evolution of European Integration

  • 1951: European Coal and Steel Community founded
  • 1957: Treaty of Rome creates the European Economic Community
  • 1993: Maastricht Treaty establishes the European Union
  • 2002: Euro currency introduced
  • 2020: Brexit - the United Kingdom leaves the EU
Texas Connection

Trade Ties: Texas and Europe

The European Union is Texas's second-largest export destination after Mexico. Key exports include petroleum products, chemicals, and electronics. German immigration significantly shaped Central Texas culture.
