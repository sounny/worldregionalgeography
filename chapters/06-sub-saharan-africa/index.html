Physical Geography

The African Plateau: Much of Sub-Saharan Africa sits on a high plateau, with dramatic escarpments along coasts.

The Great Rift Valley: A tectonic feature running from Ethiopia to Mozambique, home to major lakes and potential for future continental splitting.

Climate Zones: From equatorial rainforest to savanna to desert, Africa displays remarkable climate diversity.

üåê Global Theme: Development and Demographics

Sub-Saharan Africa has the world's youngest population, with a median age of about 19. By 2050, one in four humans will be African. This demographic transition presents both challenges and opportunities.

Texas Connection

Oil and Global Health

Nigerian oil imports to Texas refineries represent significant trade ties. Texas-based NGOs and medical institutions also engage extensively in African health initiatives.
