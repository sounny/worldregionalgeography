North Africa and Southwest Asia

Often called the "Middle East," this region is the birthplace of three major world religions, home to vast oil reserves, and faces critical challenges of water scarcity and political conflict.

Physical Geography

Aridity and Adaptation

The defining physical characteristic of North Africa and Southwest Asia is aridity. The scarcity of water has shaped human settlement, agriculture, and culture for millennia.

  • The Sahara: The world's largest hot desert, covering much of North Africa.
  • The Arabian Desert: Dominates the Arabian Peninsula, influencing the nomadic Bedouin lifestyle.
  • River Valleys: The Nile, Tigris, and Euphrates rivers serve as "exotic streams" that originate in wetter regions and flow through deserts, supporting large populations.

üïå Cultural Hearth

This region is often called the "Cradle of Civilization" and is the birthplace of three major monotheistic religions: Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

üåê Global Theme: Tradition and Modernity

The region is a zone of intense contrast between deep-rooted traditions and hyper-modern development, often funded by oil wealth.

Urban Transformation

Cities like Dubai and Doha have transformed from small fishing villages to global hubs of finance, tourism, and aviation in just a few decades.

üîç Geographic Inquiry

The region's vast oil reserves have created immense wealth for some nations while others struggle with scarcity and conflict. How does geographic distribution of energy resources shape both opportunities for development and sources of geopolitical tension in North Africa and Southwest Asia?

Texas and the Middle East face similar challenges regarding water scarcity and resource management. Texas universities have collaborated with institutions in Saudi Arabia and Israel to research desalination technology, dryland agriculture, and water conservation strategies.
