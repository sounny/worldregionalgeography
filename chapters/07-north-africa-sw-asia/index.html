North Africa and Southwest Asia

Often called the "Middle East," this region is the birthplace of three major world religions, home to vast oil reserves, and faces critical challenges of water scarcity and political conflict.

Physical Geography

Aridity: This is the world's driest region, dominated by deserts including the Sahara, Arabian Desert, and Syrian Desert.

Rivers: The Nile, Tigris, and Euphrates rivers are vital lifelines in an otherwise arid landscape. Ancient civilizations arose along these waterways.

Mountains: The Atlas, Zagros, and Taurus mountains influence climate and settlement patterns.

üåê Global Theme: Oil, Water, and Conflict

This region holds approximately half of the world's proven oil reserves, making it strategically critical to the global economy. Oil wealth has transformed some nations while others struggle with poverty.

Water scarcity creates tension between nations sharing rivers. The Nile, Jordan, and Tigris-Euphrates systems all cross international boundaries.

Texas Connection

Energy Partners

Texas refineries process significant amounts of Middle Eastern crude oil. The petrochemical industries of both regions are deeply interconnected through global energy markets.
