South Asia

Physical Geography

The Himalayas: The world's highest mountain range, including Mount Everest, was formed by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian plates and continues to rise.

The Monsoon: Seasonal wind patterns bring vital rainfall from June to September. The monsoon's timing and intensity determine agricultural success or failure for hundreds of millions.

River Systems: The Ganges, Indus, and Brahmaputra rivers support dense populations and are sacred in Hindu tradition.

ğŸŒ Global Theme: Population and Climate

India recently surpassed China as the world's most populous country. South Asia's young population presents both opportunities (demographic dividend) and challenges (job creation, resources).

Climate change threatens glacial water sources, intensifies monsoon variability, and increases flooding and cyclone risks for coastal populations.

Texas Connection

Tech and Immigration

Indian immigrants are among the largest foreign-born populations in Texas, contributing significantly to the technology sector. Austin and Houston host major South Asian communities.
