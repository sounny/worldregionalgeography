South Asia

Home to nearly two billion people, South Asia is defined by the Himalayan mountains, the monsoon climate, extraordinary religious diversity, and rapid economic transformation.

Physical Geography

Mountains and Monsoons

South Asia is defined by two major physical features: the towering Himalayas to the north and the seasonal monsoon winds that drive the region's climate.

  • The Himalayas: Created by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, these are the highest mountains in the world and the source of major rivers like the Indus, Ganges, and Brahmaputra.
  • The Monsoon: A seasonal wind shift that brings heavy rains in the summer, vital for agriculture but also causing dangerous floods.

üïå RICH CULTURAL HERITAGE

Home to the Indus Valley Civilization, South Asia is a mosaic of languages, religions, and traditions. The Taj Mahal stands as a testament to the region's architectural and historical depth.

üåê Global Theme: Population and Economic Growth

South Asia is the world's most populous region. Balancing the needs of a growing population with economic development and environmental sustainability is its critical challenge.

The Urban Challenge

Rapid urbanization is straining infrastructure in megacities like Mumbai, Delhi, and Dhaka. Yet, these cities are also engines of innovation and growth.
