Physical geography focuses on the natural environment‚Äîthe physical characteristics and processes of the Earth. It examines the "stage" upon which human history unfolds.

Key topics include climate and weather patterns, landforms (geomorphology), biogeography, and the distribution of natural resources.

üë• Human Geography: The Cultural Landscape

Human geography examines the relationship between human beings and their activities in relation to the environment. It bridges the social sciences with spatial analysis.

This includes population and migration, cultural landscapes (language, religion), economic systems, and political borders.

Consider how Texas demonstrates the concept of place. Its physical characteristics include diverse landscapes‚Äîfrom the Gulf Coast wetlands to the Hill Country. Its human characteristics include a blend of Indigenous, Spanish, Mexican, and Anglo-American heritage.

Texas is also a major player in economic globalization, serving as a hub for energy, technology, and international trade (especially via the Port of Houston and the Mexican border).

Case Study

What Makes "Europe" a Region?

Europe is often treated as a distinct continent, yet it's actually a peninsula of the Eurasian landmass. The boundaries of Europe are as much cultural and historical as they are physical.

Questions to Consider:

  • Is Europe a formal, functional, or perceptual region?
  • How do historical factors shape our definition of Europe?
