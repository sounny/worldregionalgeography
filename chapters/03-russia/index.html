Russia and Central Asia

Spanning eleven time zones, Russia is the world's largest country. Together with the Central Asian republics, this region bridges Europe and Asia with vast resources, complex politics, and enduring geographic challenges.

Physical Geography

The Russian Plain: Western Russia consists of flat to rolling terrain, home to most of the population.

Siberia: The vast eastern region contains taiga forests, permafrost, and extreme continental climate.

Central Asia: Arid steppes and deserts dominate, with mountains along southern borders.

üåê Global Theme: Energy Geopolitics

Russia holds vast oil and natural gas reserves, making energy a key tool of geopolitical influence. European dependence on Russian gas has shaped regional politics for decades.

The 2022 invasion of Ukraine and subsequent sanctions have reshaped global energy markets and accelerated Europe's search for alternatives.

Texas Connection

Energy Competition

Texas and Russia both rank among the world's top oil and gas producers. As sanctions limited Russian exports, Texas LNG exports to Europe increased significantly.
