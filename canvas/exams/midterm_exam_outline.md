### Key Topics by Chapter

#### Chapter 1: Introduction

- Definition of geography and spatial thinking
- Types of regions (formal, functional, perceptual)
- Scale and its importance
- Dimensions of globalization
- Time-space compression

#### Chapter 2: Europe

- Physical geography (mountain ranges, rivers, climate)
- European Union structure and purpose
- Demographic challenges (aging population, migration)
- Cultural diversity and nationalism
- Brexit and its implications

#### Chapter 3: Russia & Post-Soviet States

- Russia's size and climate zones
- Resource geography (oil, natural gas, minerals)
- Political transitions after Soviet collapse
- Ethnic diversity and regional conflicts
- Russia's relationship with former Soviet states

#### Chapter 4: North America

- Physical regions (Rockies, Great Plains, Appalachians)
- Urbanization patterns and megalopolises
- Immigration and cultural diversity
- Environmental challenges
- US-Canada-Mexico economic relationships (USMCA)

#### Chapter 5: Latin America & Caribbean

- Colonial legacy and its modern impacts
- Tropical deforestation (Amazon)
- Urbanization and megacities
- Economic development patterns
- Cultural syncretism

#### Chapter 6: Sub-Saharan Africa

- Colonial boundaries and their legacy
- Resource extraction and the "resource curse"
- Demographic transition and population growth
- Disease and public health challenges
- Economic development and regional organizations

### Sample Questions

#### Multiple Choice (Section A)

1. Which European mountain range separates the Iberian Peninsula from France?

   - A) Alps
   - B) Pyrenees ✓
   - C) Carpathians
   - D) Apennines

2. Russia's economy is heavily dependent on exports of:
   - A) Agricultural products
   - B) Manufactured goods
   - C) Oil and natural gas ✓
   - D) Technology services

#### True/False (Section B)

1. The Sahara Desert is located in Sub-Saharan Africa. (False)
2. NAFTA has been replaced by USMCA. (True)

#### Map Identification (Section C)

_[Students will identify countries, cities, physical features on a blank map]_

- Identify: Germany, Amazon River, Moscow, Great Lakes, Congo Basin

#### Matching (Section D)

Match the term to its definition:

- A) Maquiladora → 4) Factory in Mexico near US border
- B) Apartheid → 2) South African racial segregation system
- C) Permafrost → 1) Permanently frozen ground
- D) Balkanization → 3) Fragmentation into smaller hostile states
