## Questions

### Question 1 (Multiple Choice)

Geography is best defined as:

- A) The study of maps and cartography
- B) The study of places and the relationships between people and their environments ✓
- C) The memorization of country capitals
- D) The study of weather patterns

**Feedback (Correct)**: Geography is about understanding spatial relationships between people, places, and environments.

---

### Question 2 (Multiple Choice)

Which type of region is defined by a central node with surrounding connections that decrease with distance?

- A) Formal region
- B) Functional region ✓
- C) Perceptual region
- D) Physical region

**Feedback (Correct)**: Functional regions are organized around a central focal point, like a city and its suburbs or a TV broadcast area.

---

### Question 3 (Multiple Choice)

"The Middle East" is best described as what type of region?

- A) Formal region
- B) Functional region
- C) Perceptual region ✓
- D) Economic region

**Feedback (Correct)**: The Middle East is a perceptual (vernacular) region—its boundaries are vague and based on cultural perceptions rather than formal criteria.

---

### Question 4 (True/False)

Scale in geography only refers to the relationship between map size and actual size on Earth's surface.

- True
- False ✓

**Feedback (Correct)**: Scale also refers to the level of analysis—local, regional, national, or global. The same phenomenon looks different at different scales.

---

### Question 5 (Multiple Choice)

Which is NOT a characteristic of modern globalization?

- A) Increased speed of exchange
- B) Greater intensity of connections
- C) Universal reduction of cultural diversity ✓
- D) Worldwide economic integration

**Feedback (Correct)**: Globalization does not universally reduce cultural diversity—it often leads to "glocalization," where global influences blend with local cultures.

---

### Question 6 (Multiple Choice)

The word "geography" comes from Greek words meaning:

- A) Map drawing
- B) Earth writing ✓
- C) World studying
- D) Place naming

**Feedback (Correct)**: Geography comes from the Greek "geo" (Earth) and "graphein" (to write).

---

### Question 7 (Multiple Choice)

A formal region is characterized by:

- A) A central city that dominates surrounding areas
- B) One or more common characteristics throughout the area ✓
- C) Cultural perceptions and feelings
- D) Economic connections between places

**Feedback (Correct)**: Formal regions (uniform/homogeneous regions) share common characteristics, such as language, climate, or political boundaries.

---

### Question 8 (True/False)

Spatial thinking is the ability to visualize and interpret location, distance, direction, and relationships across space.

- True ✓
- False

**Feedback (Correct)**: Spatial thinking is the core skill of geographers—analyzing patterns, relationships, and change across space.

---

### Question 9 (Multiple Choice)

Which dimension of globalization involves trade agreements, multinational corporations, and global supply chains?

- A) Cultural globalization
- B) Political globalization
- C) Economic globalization ✓
- D) Environmental globalization

**Feedback (Correct)**: Economic globalization connects economies worldwide through trade, investment, and global supply chains.

---

### Question 10 (Multiple Choice)

"Time-space compression" refers to:

- A) The reduction in travel time between places due to technology ✓
- B) The increasing time it takes to communicate globally
- C) The compression of time zones
- D) The shrinking of physical continents

**Feedback (Correct)**: Time-space compression describes how improved transportation and communication technology have reduced the effective distance between places.

---

### Question 11 (Multiple Choice)

At the global scale, population distribution shows that most people live in:

- A) Australia and the Americas
- B) Asia and coastal areas ✓
- C) Europe and Africa
- D) Mountainous regions

**Feedback (Correct)**: At the global scale, the majority of the world's population is concentrated in Asia and along coastal areas.

---

### Question 12 (True/False)

Europe is clearly defined as a continent by its distinct physical boundaries from Asia.

- True
- False ✓

**Feedback (Correct)**: Europe is actually a peninsula of the Eurasian landmass. Its boundaries are as much cultural and historical as they are physical.

---

### Question 13 (Multiple Choice)

"Glocalization" describes:

- A) The complete replacement of local cultures by global ones
- B) The adaptation of global products and ideas to local contexts ✓
- C) The isolation of local communities from global influences
- D) The globalization of local governments

**Feedback (Correct)**: Glocalization is the blending of global and local—adapting global products, ideas, and practices to fit local contexts and preferences.

---

### Question 14 (Multiple Choice)

Which of the following is an example of a functional region?

- A) The French-speaking region of Canada
- B) A metropolitan area organized around a central city ✓
- C) "The South" in the United States
- D) The Sahara Desert

**Feedback (Correct)**: Metropolitan areas are functional regions—organized around a central city node with connections (commuting, commerce) that decrease with distance.

---

### Question 15 (True/False)

Human-environment interaction is one of the key themes in geographic study.

- True ✓
- False

**Feedback (Correct)**: Human-environment interaction examines how humans modify environments and how environments influence human behavior—a central theme in geography.
